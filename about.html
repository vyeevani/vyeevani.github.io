Who Am I & What I do

Vinru

No 1 Bio

I went to high school at Mission San Jose in Fremont, California as part of the class of 2017. I now attend UC Berkeley as a freshman as an intended Computer Science major.

I'm into everything about computers and have been programming since I was in the fourth grade. I started with Python and now know Java, Ruby, PHP, JavaScript (including NodeJs, AngularJs, ExpressJS, and MongoDB), as well as the MEANS stack. I'm also well versed in nearly every power tool ever built as well as CAD software and 3d printing. I also have a very solid understanding in electrical engineering especially power control circuits using MOSFETs and driver circuits.

No 2 Experience

I've worked on an electric motorcycle team, at Cisco Systems, and on numerous science fair teams learning many skills including management and product pipelines.

My most interesting and arguably most difficult project so far has been the construction of an electric motorcycle that uses a hybrid supercapacitor/battery pack that would drastically increase the energy recovered from the regenerative braking and increase the longevity of the batteries themselves. For this project I had to learn a significant amount of power circuits and driver circuits. At Cisco Systems, I learned how to write server side code and how global projects were managed at large corporations. As part of this internship, I worked on a team that was building Cisco's unified operating system, Polaris. I wrote code to create workspaces inside this framework as a development tool and had my code passed into Cisco's official tools included in every corporate computer. I also wrote software for a team doing rapid builds of the entire Polaris system in order to track and analyze the new builds using AngularJs and NodeJs. I also have been team leader in many science fairs including the Conrad Innovation Award in which my team made it to the semi-finals at the Kennedy Space Center in Florida for our exoskeleton that used fishing line as the contracting muscle and EKG technology to sense human muscle contractions. I also competed in the Alameda County Science Fair with a graphene based electrical harvester that could generate power directly from waves using the incredible electrical properties of graphene.
